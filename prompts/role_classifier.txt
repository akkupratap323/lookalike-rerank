Classify each candidate below into exactly one role:
- "peer"      = a SOFTWARE/PRODUCT VENDOR selling a competing or adjacent offering to the
                seed's buyer type. Company SIZE DOES NOT MATTER — a tiny vendor in the same
                category is still a peer. Adjacent product categories COUNT as peer when the
                buyer strongly overlaps (e.g. a cloud data/analytics platform sold to the
                same industry's enterprises).
- "servicer"  = agency, consultancy, implementation partner, "solutions provider", or
                services firm. CRITICAL: many services firms describe themselves with
                product-like words ("offers QMS modules", "provides cloud solutions for X").
                Signals of servicer: "consulting", "services", "implementations", "partner",
                "specializing in <seed> deployments", "helps companies adopt/implement",
                built ON another vendor's platform (e.g. "Salesforce-powered solutions").
                If the description is genuinely AMBIGUOUS between product company and
                services company, classify servicer — the judge defaults to reject.
- "tool"      = product that plugs into / extends / manages the seed's platform specifically
                (app, plugin, extension, add-on, ops tool for the seed's users).
- "self"      = the seed itself, a subsidiary, or a regional variant of the seed.
- "unrelated" = different industry or different buyer entirely.

Watch for the trap: servicers and tools describe themselves USING THE SEED'S OWN WORDS,
so they sound similar. Ask: (1) does this company COMPETE with the seed or LIVE OFF the
seed's ecosystem? (2) does it sell its OWN product, or deliver services/implementations?
Own-product competitors and adjacents (any size) are peers; everything ambiguous is not.
